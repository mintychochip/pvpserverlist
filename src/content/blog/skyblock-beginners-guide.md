# Skyblock Beginners Guide: From Single Block to Empire

Skyblock is one of Minecraft's most popular survival variants. Starting on a tiny floating island with minimal resources, you must expand, automate, and eventually build a thriving empire in the sky. This guide will take you from absolute beginner to Skyblock master.

## What is Skyblock?

Skyblock strips Minecraft down to its essentials:
- **One block** (usually grass or tree) on a floating island
- **Limited starting resources** (tree, chest with basics)
- **No normal terrain** to gather resources from
- **Challenge-based progression** through unlocks

The goal? Survive, expand, and complete challenges while building an efficient island economy.

## Your First Hour: Essential Steps

### 1. Don't Break the Tree Immediately

That single tree is your lifeline. Instead of breaking it:
- **Wait for saplings** to drop from leaf decay
- **Plant immediately** in the same spot
- **Light up the area** to prevent mob spawning

**Pro tip:** Some servers give bone meal in starting chests. Use it to speed up growth.

### 2. Expand Your Platform Safely

Build bridges carefully:
- **Crouch while building** (prevents falling)
- **Build 2-wide initially** for safety
- **Light every platform** immediately
- **Fence off edges** once you have wood

### 3. Create a Cobblestone Generator

This is your infinite resource engine:

**Basic design:**
```
[Water] [Air] [Air] [Lava]
       [Hole]
```

- Water flows toward lava
- Cobblestone forms where they meet
- Mine it, repeat forever

**Advanced tip:** Look up "cobblestone generator designs" for versions that don't lose blocks.

## Early Game Priorities

### Day 1-3: Survival Basics

1. **Infinite water source** (2x2 with water in opposite corners)
2. **Mob farm** (dark room for hostile mobs to spawn)
3. **Tree farm** (automated or manual, but organized)
4. **Safe storage** (chests in a lit room)

### Week 1: Automation Foundation

- **Mob grinder** (improve your dark room with fall damage or lava)
- **Crop farms** (wheat, carrots, potatoes)
- **Animal pens** (chickens first - easy to breed, eggs = more chickens)
- **Basic trading** with wandering traders or villager conversions

### Month 1: Island Economy

- **Automated farms** (sugarcane, cactus, mob drops)
- **Villager trading hall** (emerald generation)
- **Mining dimension** access (most servers provide this)
- **Shop setup** (sell excess to other players)

## Understanding Skyblock Economy

### Primary Money Makers

1. **Mob drops** - Bones, string, gunpowder, arrows
2. **Crops** - Wheat, carrots, potatoes at scale
3. **Wood** - Once tree farm is established
4. **Rare drops** - Witch drops, wither skeleton skulls

### Value Progression

Early game → Mid game → Late game:
- **Early:** Dirt, cobblestone, basic wood
- **Mid:** Iron, redstone, automated goods
- **Late:** Diamonds, nether items, enchanted gear

## Common Skyblock Mistakes

### 1. Falling Off Your Island

**Solution:** Always crouch near edges. Build 2-wide bridges until you have ender pearls or flight.

### 2. Wasting Limited Resources

**Solution:** Think before using dirt, lava, or water. These are finite early on.

### 3. Ignoring Light Levels

**Solution:** Mobs spawn everywhere. Light every platform, even "temporary" ones.

### 4. Not Automating Early

**Solution:** Even simple automation saves hours. Prioritize farms over decoration early.

### 5. Spending Real Money Poorly

**Solution:** If buying ranks/items, prioritize:
- Fly access (quality of life)
- Island expansion (more space)
- Sell multipliers (long-term income)

## Advanced Techniques

### Zero-Tick Farms

Some crops can be forced to grow instantly using block updates. Check if your server allows this.

### Iron Golem Farms

Villagers + zombie = iron golems = iron ingots. Essential for mid-game.

### Villager Breeding

More villagers = more trades = more emeralds = more resources.

### Slime Chunk Finding

Slime balls for pistons, sticky pistons, leads. Find your chunk early.
