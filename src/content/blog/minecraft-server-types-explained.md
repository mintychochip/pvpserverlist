# Minecraft Server Types Explained

With thousands of Minecraft servers available, finding the right one can feel overwhelming. This guide breaks down every major server type so you can find your perfect multiplayer home.

## Survival Servers

The classic Minecraft experience—gather resources, build shelters, and survive the night.

### Vanilla Survival
Pure Minecraft with no gameplay modifications. Perfect for:
- Players who want the authentic experience
- Those building complex redstone contraptions
- Groups of friends who want to collaborate

### SMP (Survival Multiplayer)
Community-focused survival with added social features:
- **Land claiming** - Protect your builds from griefers
- **Economies** - Trade with other players using in-game currency
- **Community events** - Building contests, boss fights, holiday celebrations

### Hardcore Survival
One life, no respawns. When you die, you're banned until the next season reset. For players who want real stakes.

## Creative Servers

Build without limits using unlimited resources and powerful tools.

### Plot Worlds
Everyone gets their own protected plot to build on. Great for:
- Showcasing architectural skills
- Collaborative city projects
- Learning building techniques

### Free Build
Open worlds where you can build anywhere. Often include:
- **WorldEdit** - Advanced building commands
- **VoxelSniper** - Terrain sculpting tools
- **Custom heads** - Decorative player heads

## PvP Servers

Player versus player combat is the main focus.

### Factions
Team-based warfare with base building:
- Create or join factions
- Claim land and build bases
- Raid enemy factions for loot
- Form alliances and betrayals

### KitPvP
Instant combat with preset equipment:
- Choose from various kits (archer, tank, assassin)
- Fight in arenas
- Earn points to unlock better kits
- Climb leaderboards

### UHC (Ultra Hardcore)
Hardcore survival with PvP enabled:
- Natural health regeneration disabled
- Golden apples and potions only way to heal
- Last player/team standing wins
- Border shrinks over time

## Minigame Servers

Short, fun game modes perfect for quick sessions.

### Skyblock
Start on a floating island with minimal resources:
- Expand your island through challenges
- Build automated farms
- Trade with other islands
- Some servers add PvP arenas

### Bed Wars
Base building meets PvP:
- Protect your bed while destroying others'
- Collect resources from generators
- Shop for blocks, weapons, and upgrades
- Respawn until your bed is destroyed

### SkyWars
Spawn on islands, loot chests, fight to be last standing:
- Fast-paced (matches last 5-10 minutes)
- Random chest loot adds variety
- Multiple maps keep it fresh

## RPG Servers

Role-playing game elements in Minecraft.

### MMORPG
Massive multiplayer online RPG features:
- **Classes** - Warrior, Mage, Archer with unique abilities
- **Quests** - NPCs give missions with rewards
- **Dungeons** - Instanced challenges for groups
- **Progression** - Level up and improve your character

### Towny
Civilization building with politics:
- Create towns and nations
- Manage economies and taxes
- Declare wars or form alliances
- Complex political systems

## Modded Servers

Run modified versions of Minecraft with new content.

### Pixelmon
Pokémon in Minecraft:
- Catch and train Pokémon
- Battle gyms and other players
- Explore custom worlds
- Trade and evolve your team

### Tekkit/Feed The Beast
Technology-focused modpacks:
- Build complex machinery
- Automate resource gathering
- Create power networks
- Reach end-game goals

## Finding Your Server

**Ask yourself:**
1. How much time do I have? (Casual minigames vs. long-term factions)
2. Do I prefer building or fighting?
3. Solo or team play?
4. Vanilla or modified experience?
